Career Exploration

Career exploration resources help learners understand career options, labour market trends, and pathways before making education or training decisions. These may include workshops, online tools, career talks, and experiential learning opportunities.

First Nations Career Role Models (2015)
First Nations Education Steering Committee (FNESC) provides a career exploration resource that supports educators with Indigenous role model content, classroom activities, and culturally grounded learning materials that help students explore pathways and build confidence.
Future Pathways Fireside Chats
Future Pathways offers a free online video library featuring 300+ interviews with First Nations, Métis, and Inuit role models. Students can explore real career journeys by field or community, reflect through discussion prompts, and build inspiration and confidence through stories, teachings, and practical advice from Indigenous leaders across Turtle Island.
Future Ready Youth★ Not Indigenous-specific
First Work delivers an Ontario workforce development initiative supporting youth transitions from school to career through partnership-based programming, employability supports, and pathway development resources. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
Holland Self-Directed Search
Holland Self-Directed Search provides a research-based career assessment that helps students match their interests and personality to education and career pathways using the RIASEC model (Holland Codes), with personalized results that support exploration, goal setting, and career planning.
Lexia Digital Literacy Learning
Lexia offers literacy programming that supports schools with science of reading-aligned programs, professional learning, and embedded assessments to strengthen reading skills, close literacy gaps, and improve outcomes for students across all ages and grade levels.
My Career Journey — Ontario
My Career Journey is a free Ontario government tool that helps users at any stage whether working, in school, or returning to school explore education and career options. It includes a 5 minute questionnaire to generate personalized resources, a search for in-demand careers in fields like skilled trades, health care, and IT, and dedicated pathways to find postsecondary schools, Indigenous Institutes, financial assistance, and employment supports.

Courses & Handbooks

Courses and handbooks are resources designed to support learners through key education and career transitions. They may include guided curricula, facilitator resources, digital modules, or practical tools focused on topics such as post-secondary readiness, career planning, funding navigation, wellness, and self-advocacy.

NAN Student Handbook (2024)
Nishnawbe Aski Nation (NAN) provides a student transition handbook app created to support First Nations learners moving into post-secondary, with practical tools, study and time-management tips, financial aid info, cultural supports, and links to community resources.
Strengthening Pathways for First Nations Learners (2026)
Mentee worked with the Chiefs of Ontario to develop a course that strengthens culturally grounded approaches to supporting First Nations learners through key education transitions. The course uses narrative modules, curated videos, discussion prompts, and ready-to-use activities focused on belonging, readiness, independence, wellness, self-advocacy, and building networks of support.
Taking Steps to Increase my Success in Post-Secondary
ONECA provides a program to support Indigenous women and adult learners who are considering college, university, trades, or other post-secondary pathways, offering guidance that helps learners take their first steps with confidence.

Community Supports

Community supports include First Nation education offices, Indigenous employment services, friendship centres, and local organizations that provide one-on-one guidance, advocacy, referrals, and navigation support across learner transition pathways.

Aboriginal Post-Secondary Information Program (APSIP)
APSIP offers a network of Indigenous post-secondary recruiters across Ontario and Quebec that helps Indigenous learners explore programs, campus supports, services, and pathways into college, university, and Indigenous Institutes.
Employment Ontario — Provincial Supports★ Not Indigenous-specific
The Government of Ontario provides Employment Ontario as a main access point for employment supports, job training, and career counselling services that support learner transitions into work or further education. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
Indigenous Employment & Training Supports
Anishnabeg Outreach Employment & Training Inc. provides Indigenous-led employment and training supports that help learners and job seekers build skills, access funded programs, and navigate pathways into work, education, and career development.
Ontario Federation of Indigenous Friendship Centres (OFIFC)
OFIFC supports a province-wide network of Friendship Centres providing Indigenous culturally safe programs and services that can include youth supports, education navigation, referrals, and transition assistance.
Ontario Native Women's Association (ONWA)
ONWA provides culturally grounded programs and services that support wraparound needs for Indigenous learners and families during major life and education transitions.
Regional Multicultural Youth Council★ Not Indigenous-specific
The Multicultural Association of Northwestern Ontario supports a youth-led organization and drop-in centre in Thunder Bay that provides a safe space, resources, and support for children and youth, including homework help, computers, workshops, and community activities that build belonging and leadership. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.

Financial Literacy

Financial literacy resources support learners in building practical skills related to budgeting, saving, financial planning, and understanding the costs connected to education and independent living. These tools can help reduce barriers and strengthen learner readiness during key transitions.

Financial Wellness for Indigenous Peoples
RBC offers a free online course supporting financial literacy skill-building, including budgeting, saving, protecting money, borrowing basics, and long-term planning.
Indigenous Financial Wellness
AFOA Canada provides a financial wellness learning resource supporting Indigenous individuals and communities with education on budgeting, spending, saving, investing, and long-term financial decision-making.
Indigenous Peoples' Money and Youth
The Canadian Foundation for Economic Education (CFEE) offers a financial literacy learning program designed to build practical money skills for Indigenous youth through foundational learning on budgeting, saving, and everyday financial decisions.

05

Mentorship & Peer Support

Mentorship programs connect learners with peers, professionals, Elders, or role models who provide one-on-one or group guidance. These supports help build confidence, strengthen identity, and ease transitions across education and career pathways.

Generation 4 Equality Youth Engagement Project
The Native Women's Association of Canada offers a youth initiative that provides tools, resources, and supports to help Indigenous youth advance education, leadership, and career pathways.
Indigenous Mentorship Network of Ontario (IMN-O)
IMN-O is a network for Indigenous scholars, learners, and researchers in the health and well-being sector, offering mentorship opportunities and community-building supports that help participants navigate academic and professional transitions.
IMN-O Summer Institute (2025)
The Indigenous Mentorship Network of Ontario (IMN-O) hosts an annual Summer Institute that brings Indigenous graduate students, community researchers, and mentors together for connection, mentorship, skill-building, and confidence-building across academic and career pathways.
Indigenous Summer Mentorship Program
The University of Toronto Mississauga offers a mentorship-based program supporting Indigenous high school students with early post-secondary exploration, identity development, connection to Indigenous student mentors, and transition planning.
Influence Mentoring
Influence Mentoring is an online program connecting Indigenous post-secondary students with professionals in a year-long mentor and protégé relationship. Open to First Nations, Métis, Inuit, and Non-Status students enrolled anywhere in Canada, the program matches protégés with mentors in a shared field of study and includes a kickoff bootcamp, workshops, networking events, and group programming to build career capacity and opportunities.
Niijii Indigenous Mentorship Program
Lakehead University provides a hands-on mentorship program connecting Indigenous youth with Lakehead University students through classroom visits, activities, and cultural learning that builds confidence and supports post-secondary pathways.

06

Orientation Events & Activities

Orientation events and activities are often annual or seasonal opportunities that support learner through career exploration, community connection, and practical readiness. These initiatives may include career fairs, campus orientations, summer institutes, and experiential learning events delivered in-person or virtually.

DFC Orientation "Amazing Race" Activity (2025)
Dennis Franklin Cromarty High School shares a news story highlighting student experiences and practical advice for adjusting to life in Thunder Bay, including navigating the city, finding community supports, staying safe, and building confidence during the transition to school away from home.
Gaǫdadęihwahni:ya:s Transition Program
McMaster University offers a two-week summer transition program supporting Indigenous students entering their first year through community-building, cultural safety, confidence-building, and campus readiness before classes begin.
Nibwaakaawen (WISDOM) Program
Nibwaakaawen (WISDOM) is a tuition free program from George Brown Polytechnic open to Indigenous learners aged 18 and older. Funded by Employment Ontario, the six day guided online program runs in a small group setting and includes activities, self reflection exercises, and a one on one coaching session to help participants explore careers, set personal goals, write a personal values statement, and build a clear action plan for their next steps.
Mississaugas of the Credit Career Fair (2025)
Mississaugas of the Credit First Nation offers an annual, free online career fair connecting students, job seekers, and community members with mining industry employers across Canada. Participants can explore job and training opportunities, attend webinars, connect with recruiters, and upload resumes for employer review.
Oneida Annual Youth Career Fair (2025)
Oneida Nation of the Thames hosts a youth-focused career awareness event designed to connect First Nations students with post-secondary programs, training pathways, and employers through interactive booths, information sharing, and direct conversations with recruiters and organizations.
Summer Indigenous Institute
Nipissing University offers a two-week transition program for Indigenous students entering their first year of university, designed to support academic readiness, orientation, and strong relationship-based connection before the semester begins.
Western Mini University Program
Western University hosts a week-long summer camp for Indigenous youth (ages 11–17), offering hands-on learning, cultural teachings, and a campus-based experience through workshops, activities, mentors, and exploration of post-secondary and career pathways.

07

Post-Secondary Pathways

Post-secondary pathway resources support learners in exploring entry points into college, university, apprenticeships, and Indigenous Institutes. These may include bridging and preparatory programs, transition-year initiatives, and certificate programs that strengthen academic readiness, connection, and success.

Academic Bridging Program — Woodsworth College★ Not Indigenous-specific
The University of Toronto (Woodsworth College) offers a bridging pathway that supports learners who want preparation before entering university-level study, with a focus on academic readiness and confidence. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
Indigenous Institutes Consortium (IIC Ontario)
The IIC represents a provincial network of seven out of the nine Indigenous Institutes across Ontario, supporting Indigenous-led learning pathways, training programs, and wraparound supports.
Indigenous Institutes in Ontario
The government of Ontario provides an overview of Indigenous Institutes recognized under provincial regulation, supporting Indigenous-led post-secondary learning, training pathways, and student success.
Indigenous Transition Year Program (ITYP)
Lakehead University offers an eight-month preparatory program for First Nations, Métis, and Inuit learners who need academic preparation to become eligible for admission in a culturally supportive environment.
Maawndoo Kinoomaagasiwin – Get SET
Kenjgewin Teg offers the Get SET program to help adults on Manitoulin Island develop and apply communication, numeracy, interpersonal, and digital skills. The program serves learners aged 18 and older working toward employment, post-secondary, apprenticeship, secondary school, or greater independence, and includes five services: information and referral, assessment, learner plan development, training, and follow up. Services are free.
Ontario Learn and Stay Grant★ Not Indigenous-specific
The Government of Ontario offers a grant covering tuition, books, and education costs for students in eligible post-secondary programs tied to in-demand careers. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
OntarioLearn
OntarioLearn brings together all 24 of Ontario's publicly funded colleges, two Indigenous Institutes, and one Support Organization to offer shared online post-secondary courses and programs. Students can register with a local member institution and access learning from anywhere in Ontario, with 24/7 support available. Options range from micro credentials and certificates to diplomas and degrees across a wide variety of subject areas.
Professional Studies Certificate Program
Outside Looking In (OLI) offers a certificate program that supports Indigenous youth transitioning from high school into post-secondary education or employment through mentorship, skills development, and pathway planning.
Summer Bridge Camp for Indigenous Students
Loyalist College offers a bridge-style transition experience supporting Indigenous learners through culturally safe connection, relationship-building, and readiness supports for successful entry into college.
Transitions to Post-Secondary Education — Program A107★ Not Indigenous-specific
George Brown College offers a free supportive program that helps learners build readiness for post-secondary education and employment goals for those facing barriers and benefiting from structured transition support. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
University Consortium Year 1 Program
Six Nations Polytechnic offers a one-year, community-based university preparation program where students complete first-year courses in a culturally grounded environment, with transferrable credits and transition support into partner universities.

Skills Training Initiatives

Skills training initiatives are programs designed to support learners as they transition between education, training, and employment. These initiatives may include bridge-to-work programs, paid internships, cohort-based learning, mentorship, employability skills development, and sector-specific technical training.

Bridge to Employment
Indigenous Sport & Wellness Ontario offers a paid workforce development program supporting Indigenous participants across Ontario through career readiness training and pathways including technology/coding, health and wellness, and trades/green building.
First Nations and Inuit Skills Link Program
Indigenous Services Canada provides a national program supporting First Nations and Inuit youth through funded employability skills development, work experience opportunities, and career exploration supports.
Indigenous Student Internship Program
CGI offers a paid internship program for Indigenous post-secondary students, providing hands-on experience, mentorship, and skill development to support transitions into technology and professional careers.
Indigenous Youth Policy School
Indigenous Youth Roots offers a tuition-free, nine-month program supporting youth ages 18–29 through cohort learning, mentorship, and leadership development focused on policy, civic engagement, and community pathways.
IndigiTECH Program
Indigenous Friends Association offers a free 12-week online training program that supports Indigenous learners in building career pathways in technology through technical training, mentorship, and paid internship opportunities.
Trades Exploration Program
Six Nations Polytechnic's Trades Exploration is a tuition free, seven week full time program for Indigenous and non-Indigenous domestic learners aged 16 and older who are interested in skilled trades but unsure which field to pursue. Students gain introductory theory and hands on exposure in areas including shop safety, welding and metal fabrication, machinist and 3D printing, blueprint reading, woodworking, culinary arts, and soft skills for manufacturing.
Try a Trade – Home Renovation & Preventive Maintenance
Oshki-Pimache-O-Win: The Wenjack Education Institute, in partnership with Confederation College, offers a six week hands on Try a Trade program for First Nations learners focused on home renovation and preventive maintenance. Covering carpentry, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and interior and exterior finishing, the program builds practical skills as a gateway to further trades training and Oshki-Wenjack's in-community apprenticeship programs serving Nishnawbe Aski Nation communities.

Transition Guides

Transition guides are practical, research-informed resources designed to support learners through key educational and life transitions. These may include planning tools, research reports, and system-level frameworks that strengthen coordination between institutions, families, and communities.

Aanjiiwin Transition Guide (2010)
Rainbow District School Board provides a practical transition guide with strategies and supports to help First Nation, Métis, and Inuit students transition successfully between schools and into post-secondary or work, with a focus on cultural safety, relationship-building, and individualized planning.
Aboriginal Student Transitions Project (2011)
ONECA provides a research project focused on improving Indigenous student transitions into post-secondary by identifying key needs like communication, funding support, culturally relevant services, and ongoing guidance.
AES Student Transitions Protocol
Kinoomaadziwin Education Body provides a guiding protocol outlining roles, timelines, and best practices to support Anishnabek Education System (AES) student transitions across key stages, grounded in relationship-building, privacy, and student well-being.
Indigenous Education in Ontario (2025)
The Government of Ontario provides an overview of Indigenous education commitments, priorities, and system supports that can help organizations strengthen coordination, partnership planning, and transition supports.
Deepening Our Collective Understanding (2015)
Published by Nipissing University's Office of Aboriginal Initiatives, Enji giigdoyang, this 2015 report explores how success is defined for Aboriginal university students from an Indigenous perspective. Through conversations with Elders, scholars, graduate and undergraduate students, and community members, the project develops an Aboriginal centered understanding of success grounded in identity, agency, community, and cultural continuity rather than conventional measures like enrolment and graduation rates alone.
ONECA Resource Collection
ONECA provides a collection of practical tools, guides, and research to support Indigenous student success, including transition resources, wellness and mental health materials, and education counselling supports.
Transitions Hub and Toolkits
ONECA offers a transition resource hub supporting First Nations, Métis, and Inuit learners with practical tools, printable toolkits (including budgeting supports), and referrals that strengthen education pathways.
Walk in Our Moccasins Project (2010)
ONECA provides a research report on Aboriginal Education Counsellors in Ontario that documents roles, challenges, and training needs, and highlights practices that support Indigenous student retention, transitions, and graduation.
Youth in Transition: A Resource Guide for Youth and Workers (2022)
Association of Native Child and Family Services Agencies of Ontario (ANCFSAO) offers a guide supporting Indigenous youth transitioning from child welfare systems into adulthood, focused on practical navigation, wraparound supports, and building stability and independence.

Wellness & Holistic Supports

Wellness and holistic supports help learners maintain balance across mental, emotional, physical, and cultural well-being. These resources may include counselling, cultural supports, wellness programs, and tools that strengthen resilience and support successful transitions.

Centre for Innovation in Campus Mental Health — CICMH★ Not Indigenous-specific
CICMH provides tools, training, and resources to strengthen campus mental health supports and service coordination, including content relevant to Indigenous learner transitions and accessibility planning. This resource is not Indigenous-specific, though First Nations learners may still find it helpful.
Enaadmaagemgad Enizha’yaayin
Enaadmaagemgad Enizha'yaayin is a free six week program from Kenjgewin Teg designed to empower learners aged 18 and older with foundational skills for future educational success. Grounded in Anishinabek culture and traditional knowledge, the program supports participants in developing comprehensive well-being across the physical, emotional, mental, and spiritual aspects of their lives and building readiness to pursue their next steps.
First Nations Clinicians Network
Noojimo Health offers an Indigenous-owned virtual mental health clinic providing culturally safe counselling and wellness support with Indigenous care providers, including individual, family, and group sessions, with NIHB direct billing available for eligible clients.
Institutional Wellness Digital Toolkit
The IIC offers a culturally grounded wellness toolkit with guiding questions and practical tools to help institutions strengthen holistic supports for Indigenous learners.
Pathways to Wellness Online Resource for Indigenous Students
The IIC offers an online wellness course that combines Indigenous knowledge, reflection, and practical activities to help students build holistic wellness plans during post-secondary transitions.
Rivers to Success – Indspire
Rivers to Success is a free platform from Indspire guiding First Nations, Inuit, and Métis students toward bright futures. Content is organized across three streams: High School, Post-Secondary, and Career Transition, offering tips, blogs, cultural resources, and learning materials made by and for Indigenous students. The platform also connects users to upcoming events, a job board, and funding opportunities.
Supporting Indigenous Students in Their Transition (2022)
CICMH provides a recorded webinar featuring APSIP that highlights accessibility and student support services at colleges, universities, and polytechnics across Ontario and Quebec, and shares how Indigenous representatives support learners through their educational journeys and transitions.
Supporting Indigenous Students with their Transition into Post-Secondary
CICMH and IIC offers a webinar highlighting tools and culturally grounded approaches to support Indigenous learners during transitions into post-secondary education.
Support Resources for Gender-Based Violence
Developed by Manitoba Keewatinowi Okimakanak's MMIWG Liaison Unit, this free online toolkit provides self care resources for family members of Missing and Murdered Indigenous Women, Girls, and 2SLGBTQQIA+ people and survivors of gender based violence. Tools include a daily wellness journal, grounding techniques, grief support, traditional healing medicines, event planning guides, and 2SLGBTQQIA+ resources. The MMIWG2S+ Crisis Call Line is available toll free 24/7 at 1-844-413-6649.
